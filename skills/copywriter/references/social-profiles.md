# Social Profiles

Cross-platform guide for profile copy. The profile is the conversion layer.
Great content wastes its reach if the profile doesn't close. Write profile
copy *before* the first post.

## The One Rule

A stranger lands on your profile from a viral post. They have 3 seconds to
decide: follow or leave. In those 3 seconds they need to understand:

1. **What problem you solve** (or what value you provide)
2. **Who you solve it for** (the target audience)
3. **What to do next** (CTA)

Everything else is noise. Tags, emoji stacks, job title collections, vague
buzzwords ("AI-native", "growth hacker", "serial entrepreneur") all burn
the 3 seconds without answering any of these.

## Bio Structure

### The One-Liner Formula

The strongest bios compress problem + value + audience into a single line.

**Format:** `[What you're doing], [the value it provides] for [who]`

Examples:
- `building @TryTalkValue, automating all the annoying stuff for conference/event management`
- `helping lifters track progressive overload so they actually grow`
- `writing tools for indie devs who ship alone`

**What makes it work:**
- Specific verb ("building", "helping", "writing"), not a title ("Founder", "CEO")
- The value is a pain point removed, not a capability listed
- The audience is named, not implied

### The Three-Line Formula

When you have more room (IG, TikTok, LinkedIn), use three lines:

1. **What you do.** One line, specific.
2. **Who it's for.** One line, the exact audience.
3. **CTA.** One line, with the link or next step.

Example:
```
Helping new moms track their pregnancy journey
For first-time moms who hate overthinking it
→ Download the app below 👇
```

Use the one-liner on X (character limit). Use three lines on IG, TikTok,
LinkedIn where there's room.

## Display Name

The display name (the bold text, not the @handle) is searchable on most
platforms. The niche keyword has to live here, not buried in the bio.

- Bad: `Jessica`
- Bad: `Min Seon 🖤`
- Good: `Jessica | Postpartum Fitness Coach`
- Good: `Mark | Hypertrophy Coach for Lifters`
- Good: `Min Seon` (if the bio already carries the specificity)

**Format:** `[First name] | [Niche keyword + role]`

On X, the display name is shorter and less searchable than IG, so the bio
does more work. On IG and TikTok, the display name is the primary search
field, so the niche keyword must be there.

## What to Cut

**Vague buzzwords:** "AI-native", "growth OS", "agency", "serial entrepreneur",
"thought leader", "passionate about X". These explain nothing about what value
you provide. Replace with specific verbs and outcomes.

**Title stacking:** "Founder | CEO | Advisor | Speaker | Author" tells
the reader you have a lot of jobs but not what any of them do for *them*.
Pick the one that matters to your target audience.

**Emoji clutter:** One emoji max, and only if it adds meaning (like a 👇
pointing to a link). Emoji rows (🚀💡🔥✨) signal "I have nothing specific
to say so I decorated."

**@mention stacking:** Listing every company, client, or partner buries the
value proposition. If you need social proof, pick the one name your target
audience would recognize.

**Hashtags in bio:** Hashtags in bios don't help discovery on any platform.
They just waste characters.

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Instagram

- Display name is indexed for search. Niche keyword must be here.
- Bio: three lines (what / who / CTA). 150 characters max.
- Set up 5 highlight covers before the first post. Name them for the viewer:
  `Tips`, `Reviews`, `Features`, `FAQ`, `How it works`. Skip generic `About`
  or `Story`.
- Link in bio is the only clickable link. Make it count.

### X (Twitter)

- Display name: 50 characters. Keep it clean, no emoji stacks.
- Bio: 160 characters. The one-liner formula works best here.
- Pinned tweet is your second bio. Pin your best-performing or most
  representative post, not a promo.
- Header image is prime real estate. Use it to reinforce the value prop,
  not for a city skyline or abstract art.

### TikTok

- Display name is searchable. Include the niche keyword.
- Bio: 80 characters. Ultra-compressed version of the one-liner.
- Link only available with 1K+ followers (or business account). Until then,
  the bio text has to do all the work.

### LinkedIn

- Headline (below name): 220 characters. Functions like a display name +
  bio combined. Use the one-liner formula here.
- About section: longer form, but the first 2 lines are visible before
  "see more". Put the value prop in those 2 lines.
- Don't waste the headline on your job title. LinkedIn already shows your
  current role from your experience section.

## Before/After Examples

**Bad (X):**
```
Founder @TryTalkValue, AI-Native Agency for conferences and communities 🖤
Running socials+communities for @KGConference, @sigma2society, events for @IAEE_HQ +a
```
Problems: "AI-Native Agency" means nothing to the reader. @mention stacking.
Emoji. No clear value prop.

**Good (X):**
```
building @TryTalkValue, automating all the annoying stuff for conference/event management
```
Why it works: specific verb, specific pain point, specific audience. One line.

**Bad (IG):**
```
🏋️ Fitness enthusiast 💪
🇸🇪 Stockholm
📧 DM for collabs
```
Problems: "fitness enthusiast" is everyone. Emoji-led lines. No value, no
audience, no CTA that matters.

**Good (IG):**
```
Science-based hypertrophy for lifters who want to grow
Training breakdowns backed by actual studies
→ GainsLog: track your lifts 👇
```
Why it works: specific niche, specific value, clear CTA.

## The Test

Read your bio as a stranger. Can you answer these three questions?

1. What does this person/product do for me?
2. Am I the right audience?
3. What should I do next?

If any answer is unclear, rewrite. If you need more than 10 seconds to figure
it out, the bio failed.
