# B2C Mobile Apps

Consumer mobile is its own game. The audience is distracted, scrolling, and
deciding in milliseconds whether your app is worth a tap. The single most
important lesson: **dumb everything down.**

This is what makes apps both viral and high-converting. Sophistication loses.
Obviousness wins.

## The Three Rules

### 1. Show value in 3 seconds or less

Any advertising material - App Store screenshots, ads, video hooks, landing
hero, push previews - has to communicate the value in under 3 seconds. If a
viewer can't tell what they get out of it before their thumb moves, it's dead.

Test it: glance at the asset for 3 seconds, look away. Can you say what the
app does for you? If not, cut, simplify, or rewrite.

### 2. Write for a 3rd grader

Short words. Short sentences. No jargon, no industry language, no clever
phrasing that requires a second read. If a 9-year-old wouldn't understand it,
rewrite it.

This isn't about treating users as stupid. It's about removing every
millisecond of friction between seeing the words and getting the point.
Smart people scrolling fast read like 3rd graders.

| Too smart | 3rd-grader version |
|-----------|--------------------|
| "Optimize your nutrition" | "Eat better" |
| "Track macronutrients effortlessly" | "Snap a photo. See the calories." |
| "Maximize your training output" | "Lift more. Every week." |

### 3. Buttons so obvious you can't get lost

Button labels should tell the user exactly what happens when they tap. No
cleverness. No branded verbs. No ambiguity.

- "Get Started" beats "Begin Your Journey"
- "Log Workout" beats "New Entry"
- "See My Plan" beats "Continue"

If a user has to think about which button to tap, the button has failed.
The path through the app should feel like the only possible path.

## Why This Matters

Viral consumer apps and high-converting paywalls share the same trait: a
5-year-old could use them. The product can be sophisticated under the hood.
The surface has to be brain-dead simple.

When in doubt, dumb it down further than feels comfortable. You will almost
never go too simple.
