# Slideshow / Carousel

Guide for writing static-stack copy: TikTok slideshows and Instagram
carousels. Functionally the same format — a stack of static images with
text, swiped or auto-advanced. Same rules apply on both platforms, so
this reference covers them together.

A static stack is one of the highest-leverage organic formats: zero
production cost, the reader controls pace (so dwell time is high), and
replays/swipe-backs are automatic when the hook is strong.

For caption / post description copy that pairs with the stack, see
[ig-captions.md](./ig-captions.md). For short-form video (Reels and
TikTok video), see [short-form-video.md](./short-form-video.md).

## Why Static Stacks Win

- Zero production cost. Images from Pinterest or stock libraries, text
  overlaid in any editor.
- Readers control the pace, so they dwell longer than on video.
- Replays / swipe-backs are automatic — if the hook is strong, viewers
  go back to slide 1.
- Time-on-post and replays are the two strongest signals to the
  algorithm on both TikTok and Instagram.

## Slide Structure

A stack has three jobs across three slide groups. Lock this structure.

```
Slide 1         → Hook. Stop the scroll.
Slides 2..N-1   → Payload. Deliver on the hook's promise.
Slide N         → Close + CTA. Validation line, then the handoff.
```

Typical length: 5-8 slides total. A "5 things..." hook is exactly 1 hook
slide + 5 payload slides + 1 close slide = 7 slides.

## Slide 1: The Hook

The first slide decides reach. The first 3 seconds are all you get.

**Rules:**
- **Under 10 words.** If it doesn't fit, rewrite it.
- **Question or strong statement.** Not both. Not neither.
- **One line, high contrast.** Readable at a glance, no squinting.
- **First text overlay must be the hook itself.** Not a logo, not a greeting,
  not "Part 1". The hook is the first thing the eye lands on.
- **No generic openers.** Skip "Did you know", "Let me tell you", "Welcome".

**Three emotional triggers to pick from** (pick one, don't mix):

1. **Curiosity.** "5 signs your body is doing the right thing"
2. **FOMO / warning.** "Things nobody tells you about X"
3. **Empathy / reassurance.** "It's normal if you feel X"

Reassurance is especially strong for health, parenting, and
emotional-stakes niches. Warning is strongest in how-to and fitness niches.

**Hook formats that repeat across niches:**
- `"[Number] things [target audience] never tells you about [topic]"`
- `"You're doing [X] wrong"`
- `"I did [X] for [duration] and here's what happened"`
- `"[N] signs [bad thing] is actually [good thing]"`
- `"What [authority figure] won't tell you about [topic]"`

See [ig-captions.md](./ig-captions.md) for the full hook formula bank —
everything that works for Reels captions works for slide 1.

## Slides 2 to N-1: The Payload

Each middle slide is one point, one screen. No paragraphs.

**Rules:**
- **One insight per slide.** If a slide has two points, split it.
- **Match the count to the hook.** "5 things" = exactly 5 payload slides.
  Never 4, never 6.
- **Arrow prefix works.** `→ [point]` reads faster than bullet dots.
- **Lead with the specific.** The first 3-6 words should carry the
  actual insight. "Fatigue often means your body is redirecting energy"
  beats "One thing to know is that fatigue..."
- **Expand with the mechanism, not filler.** Give the *why* in one
  additional sentence. Then stop.
- **No emoji stacks.** One emoji max per slide, and only if it
  reinforces meaning.

**Slide length target:** 1-3 short sentences. If the reader has to scroll
within a single slide, it's too long.

## Slide N: The Close

Two lines, in order:

1. **Validation or reframe.** Something that lands the feeling, ties the
   payload back to the reader. Example: "You don't need to enjoy
   pregnancy to be doing it right."
2. **CTA.** The handoff to your app, profile, or follow.

Never end on a bullet. Never end on "thanks for watching". The close
earns the follow or the tap.

## On-Slide CTA Placement

For app marketing, the CTA lives in three places at once
([ig-captions.md](./ig-captions.md) has the same rule for Reels):

1. **Final slide** as the main message
2. **Small corner overlay** on the hook slide (so scroll-stoppers who
   don't read to the end still see it)
3. **Bio** with a direct download link

Don't cram the CTA onto every slide. It trains the reader to ignore it.

## Platform Notes

The format is identical on both platforms, but a few small differences
matter:

- **Slide count.** TikTok caps at 35 images, IG carousels at 20. Both
  formats land best at 5-8 regardless of cap.
- **Auto-advance vs swipe.** TikTok slideshows auto-advance with timing
  per slide; IG carousels are user-swiped. Keep slide text short enough
  to read in 2-3 seconds either way — the auto-advance pace sets the
  ceiling.
- **Cover frame.** IG lets you set a cover image separate from slide 1;
  TikTok shows slide 1 as the cover. Treat slide 1 as the cover for both
  to keep the same asset working cross-platform.
- **Aspect ratio.** Use 1080×1350 (4:5) — works on both, doesn't crop
  the hook on either.

## Images

The text carries the message. The image carries the aesthetic. Match
the niche, don't overthink it.

- **Pinterest is the source.** Search aesthetic + niche keywords
  ("minimal pregnancy", "dark mode gym", "pastel study desk") and pull
  images that match your account's visual identity.
- **Consistency > variety.** The same visual style across all posts
  compounds recognition. A stranger should be able to identify your
  posts in the feed without reading the text.
- **High contrast with the text overlay.** If the image is busy, add a
  dark or light rectangle behind the text. Readability wins every time.
- **AI-generated images tend to underperform** against curated real
  photography in most niches. Curate first, generate only if nothing
  fits.

## The Mining Workflow

Never invent stack formats from scratch. Mine what's already working.

1. **Find viral stacks in the niche.** Search TikTok and Instagram for
   niche keywords, filter by Most Liked or Most Recent.
2. **Save the hooks that repeat.** If you see the same format three
   times from different accounts, it's a pattern worth copying.
3. **Analyze one winner in depth.** For a stack that's clearly viral,
   break down: what's the hook, why does it work (curiosity / FOMO /
   empathy), what's the structural pattern.
4. **Generate 5-10 variations of the hook** for your niche, same
   structure, different angle. This is the agent's job when running
   this skill — don't hand it back to the user.
5. **Build a hook library.** Save winners to a running file so future
   posts start from proven patterns instead of a blank page.

## Analyzing a Viral Stack

When the user shares a slideshow or carousel to learn from, run this
analysis:

1. **The hook.** Quote the first-slide text verbatim.
2. **Why it works.** Pick one: curiosity / FOMO / empathy / warning /
   reassurance. Explain in one sentence.
3. **Structural pattern.** Extract the reusable skeleton
   (e.g. `"[number] [things] [target audience] never tells you about [topic]"`).
4. **Variations.** Generate 5-7 hooks in the same structure, different
   angles, tuned to the user's niche. Under 10 words each.
5. **Pinterest search terms.** Suggest 5-10 short phrases that match
   the visual style of the reference stack — aesthetic keywords,
   composition notes, niche-specific visuals.

## Cadence

Lock the format, change the hook theme. 1-2 posts per day while growing,
1/day once growth is compounding. Same structure, new angle, repeat.

When a post hits, generate more variations on that winning hook before
moving to the next pattern. Keep mining the winner until it stops working.

## Don't

- Don't change the format post to post. Lock the slide count and rhythm.
- Don't put the CTA on every slide.
- Don't write walls of text per slide. One insight, one screen.
- Don't use stock inspirational lines ("stay consistent", "trust the process").
  The payload has to be niche-specific and actually new.
- Don't skip slide N. An abrupt end kills the CTA.
- Don't count the slides wrong. "5 things" = 5 payload slides. Not 4, not 6.
